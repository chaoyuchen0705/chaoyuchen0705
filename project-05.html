Demographic Analysis and Population Projection of San Francisco-Oakland Berkeley Metropolitan Statis

In this project, I explored the regional population change trends and its future development potential through an in-depth analysis of the population data of the San Francisco-Oakland-Berkeley metropolitan statistical area (SOB-MSA). The study mainly covers the comparison of the population pyramid data in 2010 and 2020, revealing the continuous growth of the young labor force (ages 20-29) and the significant upward trend of the aging population. Using linear regression and other methods, we forecasted the region's future population growth and found that by 2050, the SOB-MSA's population could exceed 7 million. This growth is due to its favorable climate conditions, leading position in innovative technology, and strong economic appeal.

The project combines historical data, quantitative models and visualization results to highlight the diversity of the region's population structure and its positive impact on economic development. The research results not only provide decision-making support for urban planning and resource allocation, but also reflect potential changes in housing demand, traffic pressure and the demand for social services. This provides an important reference for optimizing urban development strategies and addressing the challenges of population change in the future.
